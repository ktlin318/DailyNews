# Content policy

## Factual fidelity

- Preserve exact numbers, signs, units, currencies, dates, times, timezones, comparison periods, and seasonal-adjustment status.
- Never convert, round, extrapolate, annualize, or calculate unless explicitly labeled and source values are preserved.
- Attribute forecasts, opinions, estimates, and issuer claims.
- Distinguish preliminary data from final data.
- If sources conflict, list both claims for manual review and omit the disputed value publicly.

## Writing style

- Use Traditional Chinese and neutral, compact language.
- Write factual titles, one- or two-sentence summaries, and one-sentence impact statements.
- Avoid promises such as `必然`, `確定上漲`, `最佳買點`, and `穩賺`.
- Use inference language only when clearly supported and labeled.

## Event grouping

- Merge only when actor, action, subject, and event time materially match.
- Keep follow-up decisions, filings, results, and market reactions separate when they are new events.
- Format stable IDs as `YYYY-MM-DD-short-kebab-slug`.

Always flag inaccessible pages, single-source market claims, promotional ETF material, conflicting figures, uncertain times, and interpretation-heavy summaries.
